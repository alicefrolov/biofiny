01 — The Enemy

12 Hallmarks of Aging

01

Genomic Instability

Accumulated DNA damage disrupts cellular function, driving mutations and systemic dysfunction over decades.

02

Telomere Attrition

Protective caps on chromosomes shorten with every cell division, eventually triggering senescence or apoptosis.

03

Epigenetic Alterations

Gene expression patterns drift from their youthful state, reprogrammable via Yamanaka factor interventions.

04

Loss of Proteostasis

Protein folding and clearance machinery declines, leading to toxic aggregations linked to neurodegeneration.

05

Disabled Macroautophagy

The cell's self-cleaning recycling system degrades, allowing cellular debris to accumulate and impair function.

06

Deregulated Nutrient Sensing

mTOR, AMPK, and insulin signaling pathways lose calibration, accelerating metabolic aging across tissues.

07

Mitochondrial Dysfunction

The cell's power plants accumulate damage, producing oxidative stress that amplifies all other hallmarks.

08

Cellular Senescence

Zombie cells accumulate throughout the body, secreting inflammatory signals that degrade surrounding tissues.

09

Stem Cell Exhaustion

Regenerative capacity declines as stem cell pools deplete and lose functional properties needed for tissue repair.

10

Altered Intercellular Communication

Signaling between cells degrades with age, disrupting coordinated tissue function and immune surveillance.

11

Chronic Inflammation

Low-grade inflammaging driven by senescent cells and immune dysfunction accelerates every other hallmark.

12

Dysbiosis

Gut microbiome imbalance disrupts metabolism, immunity, and systemic signaling, contributing to accelerated aging.

02 — The Arsenal

Current Interventions

Senolytics

Senolytics selectively eliminate senescent cells — the "zombie cells" that accumulate with age, corrupting surrounding tissue through the senescence-associated secretory phenotype (SASP).

Dasatinib + Quercetin, Navitoclax, and Fisetin are the leading compounds, with multiple clinical trials now underway for age-related diseases.

StatusClinical Trials
Target HallmarkCellular Senescence
Animal DataStrong
Human DataEmerging

Dasatinib + Quercetin

First senolytic combination shown to clear senescent cells in humans. Intermittent dosing reduces systemic senescent cell burden measurably.

Navitoclax (ABT-263)

BCL-2 family inhibitor that selectively induces apoptosis in senescent cells by targeting their anti-apoptotic survival mechanisms.

Fisetin

Naturally occurring flavonoid with strong senolytic properties. Lower toxicity profile makes it promising for long-term intermittent use.

Epigenetic Reprogramming

Partial expression of Yamanaka factors can reverse epigenetic aging marks without full dedifferentiation — restoring youthful gene expression patterns in aged cells.

Considered the most transformative longevity approach by many researchers, as it targets upstream information loss rather than downstream damage.

StatusPreclinical
TargetEpigenetic Clock
Animal DataVery Strong
RiskTumorigenesis

OSK Reprogramming — Altos Labs

$3B company developing safe partial reprogramming using Oct4, Sox2, Klf4 to restore youthful cellular identity without cancer risk.

Chemical Reprogramming

Small molecule cocktails inducing reprogramming without viral vectors — safer and more scalable for eventual therapeutic delivery.

In Vivo Reprogramming

AAV-delivered reprogramming factors directly into living organisms — the ultimate goal for systemic whole-body rejuvenation.

mTOR Inhibition

Rapamycin and its analogs inhibit mTORC1, mimicking caloric restriction's effects on aging. The most consistent lifespan extension data in model organisms of any single known intervention.

StatusOff-Label Use
TargetNutrient Sensing
Animal DataExcellent
Human DataPromising

Rapamycin

mTORC1 inhibitor extending mouse lifespan by 9–14% even when started late in life. Growing human longevity community uses intermittent dosing protocols.

Rapalogs

Second-generation mTOR inhibitors with improved selectivity, aiming to preserve longevity benefits while reducing immunosuppressive side effects.

NAD+ Restoration

NAD+ levels decline dramatically with age, impairing sirtuin activity, DNA repair, and mitochondrial function. Precursors NMN and NR can measurably restore levels in human tissue.

StatusSupplements + Trials
TargetMitochondria + DNA Repair
Animal DataStrong
SafetyExcellent

NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide)

Direct NAD+ precursor showing improvements in muscle function, insulin sensitivity, and NAD+ blood levels in human clinical trials.

NR (Nicotinamide Riboside)

Alternative NAD+ precursor with robust safety data and demonstrated ability to raise NAD+ in blood and multiple tissues.

04 — Questions

Critical Questions

Is defeating aging actually scientifically possible?

+
The scientific consensus has shifted dramatically. Aging is now understood as a biological process driven by identifiable molecular mechanisms — not a fixed law of nature. We have already reversed aging in model organisms. The 12 hallmarks provide a comprehensive mechanistic framework. The question is no longer "can we" but "how fast can we move."

What is longevity escape velocity?

+
Longevity escape velocity (LEV) is the point at which science extends human life expectancy faster than time passes. If you gain more than one year of healthy life expectancy for every year lived, you escape the mortality curve. Aubrey de Grey estimates LEV is achievable within decades with sufficient scientific investment.

Will AI dramatically accelerate longevity research?

+
AlphaFold's protein structure predictions transformed structural biology overnight. Similar breakthroughs in aging research could compress decades of work into years. AI systems are already identifying novel drug candidates, predicting treatment outcomes, and designing trials. The convergence of AI with aging biology may be the decisive catalyst.

Which single intervention has the greatest impact today?

+
Epigenetic reprogramming is considered most transformative as it addresses upstream information loss. Senolytics offer the clearest near-term clinical benefit. mTOR inhibition has the most consistent animal data. The current leading hypothesis is a combination approach: senolytics, rapamycin, NAD+ precursors, and epigenetic interventions together.
