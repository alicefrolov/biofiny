01 — The Enemy

The 12 Hallmarks
of Aging

01

Genomic Instability

DNA damage accumulates over time from environmental and metabolic sources, driving mutations that impair cellular function.

02

Telomere Attrition

Protective caps on chromosomes shorten with each cell division, eventually triggering senescence or apoptosis.

03

Epigenetic Alterations

Patterns of gene expression drift away from youthful states, silencing critical genes and activating harmful ones.

04

Loss of Proteostasis

The system that folds, maintains and clears proteins deteriorates, allowing toxic aggregates to accumulate in cells.

05

Disabled Macroautophagy

The cellular self-cleaning system that recycles damaged components loses efficiency with age.

06

Deregulated Nutrient Sensing

Key metabolic pathways including mTOR, AMPK and sirtuins lose proper calibration, accelerating aging processes.

07

Mitochondrial Dysfunction

The powerhouses of cells accumulate damage, producing less energy and more harmful reactive oxygen species.

08

Cellular Senescence

Damaged cells that should die instead persist and secrete inflammatory signals that corrupt surrounding tissue.

09

Stem Cell Exhaustion

The regenerative capacity of tissues declines as stem cell pools deplete and lose their functional properties.

10

Altered Intercellular Communication

Signaling between cells degrades with age, disrupting coordinated tissue function and immune surveillance.

11

Chronic Inflammation

Low-grade systemic inflammation — inflammaging — driven by senescent cells and immune dysfunction accelerates all other hallmarks.

12

Dysbiosis

Imbalance in the gut microbiome disrupts metabolism, immunity, and systemic signaling contributing to accelerated aging.

02 — The Arsenal

Current Interventions

Senolytics

Senolytics selectively eliminate senescent cells — the "zombie cells" that accumulate with age, corrupting surrounding tissue through the senescence-associated secretory phenotype (SASP).

Dasatinib + Quercetin, Navitoclax, and Fisetin represent the current leading senolytic compounds, with multiple clinical trials underway for age-related diseases.

Status Clinical Trials
Target Hallmark Cellular Senescence
Animal Data Strong
Human Data Emerging

Dasatinib + Quercetin

First senolytic combination shown to clear senescent cells in humans. Intermittent dosing protocol reduces systemic senescent cell burden.

Navitoclax (ABT-263)

BCL-2 family inhibitor that selectively induces apoptosis in senescent cells by targeting their anti-apoptotic survival mechanisms.

Fisetin

Naturally occurring flavonoid with senolytic properties. Lower toxicity profile makes it promising for long-term intermittent use.

Epigenetic Reprogramming

Partial expression of Yamanaka factors (Oct4, Sox2, Klf4, c-Myc) can reverse epigenetic aging marks without causing full dedifferentiation — restoring youthful gene expression patterns.

Considered the most transformative longevity approach by many researchers, as it targets upstream information loss rather than downstream damage.

StatusPreclinical
TargetEpigenetic Clock
Animal DataVery Strong
RiskTumorigenesis

Altos Labs — OSK Reprogramming

$3B company developing safe partial reprogramming using Oct4, Sox2, Klf4 factors to restore youthful cellular identity.

Chemical Reprogramming

Small molecule cocktails capable of inducing reprogramming without viral vectors — safer and more scalable delivery.

In Vivo Reprogramming

Delivering reprogramming factors directly into living organisms via AAV vectors — the ultimate goal for systemic rejuvenation.

mTOR Inhibition

Rapamycin and its analogs inhibit mTORC1, mimicking caloric restriction effects on aging. The most consistent life extension data in model organisms of any intervention.

StatusOff-Label Use
TargetNutrient Sensing

Rapamycin

mTORC1 inhibitor extending lifespan in mice by 9-14% even when started late in life. Growing human longevity community uses it at low intermittent doses.

Rapalogs

Second-generation mTOR inhibitors with improved selectivity profiles aiming to preserve benefits while reducing immunosuppression side effects.

NAD+ Restoration

NAD+ levels decline dramatically with age, impairing sirtuin activity, DNA repair, and mitochondrial function. Precursors NMN and NR can restore levels in humans.

StatusSupplements + Trials
TargetMitochondria + DNA Repair
Animal DataStrong
SafetyExcellent

NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide)

Direct NAD+ precursor showing improvements in muscle function, insulin sensitivity, and NAD+ levels in human trials.

NR (Nicotinamide Riboside)

Alternative NAD+ precursor with strong safety data and demonstrated ability to raise NAD+ levels in blood and tissues.

04 — Questions

Critical Questions

Is defeating aging actually scientifically possible?

+
The scientific consensus has shifted dramatically. Aging is now understood as a biological process driven by identifiable molecular mechanisms — not a fixed law of nature. We have already reversed aging in model organisms. The 12 hallmarks provide a comprehensive mechanistic framework. The question is no longer "can we" but "how fast can we move."

What is longevity escape velocity?

+
Longevity escape velocity (LEV) is the point at which science is extending human life expectancy faster than time passes. If you gain more than one year of healthy life expectancy for every year you live, you escape the mortality curve. Aubrey de Grey estimates LEV is achievable within decades with sufficient scientific investment.

Will AI dramatically accelerate longevity research?

+
AlphaFold's protein structure predictions transformed structural biology overnight. Similar AI breakthroughs in aging research could compress decades of work into years. AI systems are already identifying novel drug candidates, predicting treatment outcomes, and designing clinical trials. The convergence of AI with aging biology may be the key catalyst.

What are the ethical implications of radical life extension?

+
Profound questions arise: Will it only be available to the wealthy? How would overpopulation be managed? Proponents argue longevity would reduce suffering and allow accumulated wisdom. Equitable access and societal adaptation are as important as the science itself. These are problems worth having.

Which single intervention has the greatest impact?

+
Epigenetic reprogramming is considered most transformative as it addresses upstream information loss. Senolytics offer the clearest near-term clinical benefit. mTOR inhibition has the most consistent animal data. A combination approach — senolytics, rapamycin, NAD+ precursors, epigenetic interventions — is the current leading hypothesis for near-term maximum benefit.
